ABOUTME

I am a researcher focused on digital and smart construction. I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Faculty of Construction and Environment at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, where I am part of the Smart Construction Lab research group.

I obtained my BSc and MSc from Tsinghua University, which is the best engineering school in China and one of the best in the world. In 2017, I was awarded a Hong Kong PhD Fellowship which is a very prestigious scholarship given to world-class young scientists to do PhD studies in Hong Kong institutions. Under the supervision of Professor Heng Li at Hong Kong Polytechnic University, I started a cutting-edge research programme on combining computer vision and sensor technologies to investigate physical and mental fatigues of site workers. During my Ph.D. study, I developed a series of methodologies and models to analyse root causes of fatigues. My research results have been published in over ten papers in top journals of the construction management field. My work is recognized by the academic community as it is evidenced by two best paper awards from the 35th International Symposium on Automation and Robotics in Construction (ISARC 2018) and the Chartered Institute of Building (Hong Kong) in 2018; respectively. I am a firm believer of multi-disciplinary research.

To learn more about my background, please see my curriculum vitae.
